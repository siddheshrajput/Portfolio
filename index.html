Vision Inspection System

Graduate Design Project
August 2018- May 2019

UNC Charlotte

What is it?

My graduate design project- Vision Inspection System supported by Prototyping Department of Schaeffler Group USA, Fort Mill, SC.  I worked in a cross-functional team of 7 engineering students to design, program, fabricate, assemble and test a vision inspection system.

The major function of the machine is to inspect the defect in the parts and sort them into different bins. Thereby, minimizing the efforts made by quality technician and reducing overall cost and time of the production cycle.

What did I do?

  1. Designed CAD models of various components of the electro-mechanical and sorting sub-system using Creo Parametric (Pro/Engineer) to incorporate within the assembly. Prepared 2D and 3D drawings using geometric dimensioning and tolerancing (GD&T).
  2. Handled 3D printing operation for reducing time, cost and make easy modifications.
  3. Chose quality material to reduce cost by 60% and performed manual stress/strain calculations. Performed manual stress/strain calculations on the parts and finite element analysis on CAD models on ABAQUS to evaluate von Mises Stress and deformation. 
  4. Generated purchasing orders and bill of materials for the electro-mechanical sub-system.
  5. Performed calculations for selecting linear motion drive and stepper motors to meet customer specification and requirements.
  6. Communicated and coordinated with team members, mentor and industry supporter to deliver the parts of the desired product in time.

Analysis of Bar Fixed at One End

Finite Element Analysis Project- May 2018

• Developed a MATLAB code for one-dimensional analysis of a bar fixed at one end and free at left end to take user input and perform modal analysis using Gauss Quadrature rule and dynamic analysis using a Heaviside function.

• Determined the natural frequencies using Exact method and lumped mass matrix formulation to compare the eigenvalues.
